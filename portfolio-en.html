FNDE · complementary case

Bank transaction auditing with Random Forest

A machine learning project built as a close technical example of the kind of problem I faced at FNDE: prioritizing inconsistencies and risk signals in transactional data to support audit, analysis and decision-making.

  • Goal: classify suspicious transactions in an audit scenario using a synthetic public dataset while preserving the problem logic without exposing real data.
  • Pipeline: Parquet reading, temporal parsing, derived features, preprocessing with ColumnTransformer, imputation, OneHotEncoder and RandomForestClassifier training.
  • Features: transaction amount, type, location, device, time window, international transaction and derived attributes such as day_of_week, month, sender_prefix and receiver_prefix.
  • Metrics: F1-score = 0.9620, ROC-AUC = 0.9996 and PR-AUC = 0.9967 on the current split.
  • Interpretability: feature importance used to rank relevant audit signals such as amount, transaction type, location and device.
  • Technical highlight: supervised classification on tabular data, imbalanced-data handling, feature-importance explainability and adaptation of a synthetic case to a real context.

EVCOMX - Petrobras · technical reproduction

LLM Tag Extraction Lab for technical extraction with human validation

Python project created to reproduce a type of problem I worked on at EVCOMX for Petrobras: extracting technical tags from noisy texts by comparing a rigid baseline with more robust approaches based on fuzzy matching, LangChain, few-shot prompting and human review.

  • Business problem: technical texts arrive with abbreviations, spelling noise and naming variations, but the system still needs to recover the canonical tag correctly.
  • Approaches compared: regex/dictionary, RapidFuzz for lexical retrieval and LangChain few-shot with TF-IDF plus cosine similarity and an optional external-LLM path.
  • Benchmark: 300 evaluated examples, with a clear gain when moving from rigid rules to contextual approaches.
  • Results: regex with F1 = 0.4628, fuzzy with F1 = 0.7465 and langchain_llm with F1 = 0.9639 and exact match = 0.9083.
  • Technical highlight: safe evolution from regex to LLMs, the role of fuzzy matching and the design of human validation for continuous improvement.

Presidency of the Republic

Linear regression baseline for COVID-19 deaths in Brazil

Project developed from official open data to model the trend of COVID-19 deaths in Brazil using a classic, interpretable and reproducible baseline. The proposal transformed government microdata into a daily time series and evaluated the predictive capacity of a linear model with temporal feature engineering.

  • Context: aligned with my internship at the Presidency, connecting public-data analysis, health topics and applied quantitative modeling.
  • Data source: official SIVEP-Gripe / SRAG microdata published in the SUS open-data portal.
  • Pipeline: filtering COVID-19 death cases, national daily aggregation, full 2024 calendar reindexing and target-series construction with a 7-day moving average.
  • Metrics: R² = 0.9420, MAE = 0.4576 and RMSE = 0.5639.
  • Technical highlight: transformation of public microdata into a usable time series, use of linear regression as a baseline and interpretable evaluation.
